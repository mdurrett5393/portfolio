# *I DON’T READ MUCH* 📖 *(CLEARLY)*
![SpongeBob reading a field guide](assets/spongebob.gif)

_Yes, you read that title correctly_—I read a whole **five** books this year. _For **me**_, that’s as good as it gets. I also had a handful I didn’t finish, and I have some great books to read in 2025.

I tend to go for difficult books, either in length, subject matter, or both. I also tend to stick to nonfiction, specifically in the areas of biography, social science, psychology, anthropology, and other similar disciplines. I love learning more about real people, things, and ideas.

By the way: All but one of these books were ones I borrowed from the library. If you don’t already use [Libby](https://libbyapp.com/), what are you doing? It’s one of the best things your local library has available to you. One of the perks of going back to community college has been the wider access to more audiobooks, which I think is definitely the best format for me now.

Let’s get into my list! ⬇️

# *BOOKS I FINISHED* ✅
![The Will to Change - bell hooks](assets/the-will-to-change.jpg)

## _The Will to Change by Bell Hooks_

### Finished: 03/07/2024

### **Format: 💻 E-book**

This was my **first** Bell Hooks book, but it most certainly _won’t be my last_. What can be said about her that hasn’t already been said? This is a fantastic read to understand her unique perspective of men in the discussion of **patriarchy**, **masculinity**, and **relationships**.

She doesn’t attack anyone—she points out how many of the power structures in place benefit a select few, and how men performing that brand of masculinity are also victims in that pursuit. I can’t wait to read more of her incredible writing.

### **5 (patriarchies sucking real bad for everyone) out of 5**

![Unmasking Autism - Devon Price](assets/unmasking-autism.jpg)
## _Unmasking Autism by Devon Price, PhD_

### Finished: 05/13/2024

### Format: 💻 E-book

In my ongoing self-exploration, I wanted to get some perspective on autism. Dr. Price discusses the difficulties of late diagnosis, how to find community, and many other frameworks _to understand what autism isn’t as much as what it really is_. As you would expect, a book written by people with autism for people with autism has plenty of worksheets and other resources to use as a companion with the book.

As an undiagnosed, but likely autistic person, I found this to be very _insightful_ and _valuable_, even if I know a lot of the things I read in this may not be things I’m ready to accept just yet. **These things take time.**

![Elon Musk - Walter Isaacson](assets/elon-musk.jpg)
## _Elon Musk by Walter Isaacson_

### Finished: 08/11/2024

### Format: 🎧 Audiobook

As one of the richest people that’s ever existed, Elon is…an _interesting_ _person_ to say the least. Isaacson paints an in-depth picture as always, but his track record of genius worship doesn’t lend itself to authentic objectivity. It did come out a bit before his recent political…_“pivot”_, but I can’t help but feel like it was a strategic move on Musk’s part (both the pivot and book release, to be clear).

This has given me a couple of different conclusions: **soft ball journalism has no word limit**. **Information, no matter how objective, can be biased in _subtle_ ways**. At the end of the day, I learned more about who Elon is (and isn’t), but I didn’t find much of it very surprising.

### 4 (hundred billion dollars, but somehow zero actual friends) out of 5

![Comedy Drama - Bob Odenkirk](assets/comedy-drama.jpg)
## _Comedy Comedy Comedy Drama by Bob Odenkirk_

### Finished: 10/21/2024

### Format: 🎧 Audiobook

Like a lot of people my age, my first time seeing Bob in anything was his amazing work as Saul Goodman in Breaking Bad and the subsequent prequel series Better Call Saul. As the title suggests, his passion and focus has been **much** more _skewed_ towards comedy throughout his career, and learning about his connection to many different comedy legends that I knew of—and a lot that I didn’t—was a super fun journey to be on.

_His written voice read by his spoken voice is a real treat, and I don’t think anyone else could have done it justice._

### 4 (times I had no idea who he was talking about) out of 5
![Creativity, Inc. - Ed Catmull](assets/creativity-inc.jpg)
## _Creativity, Inc. by Ed Catmull_

### Finished: 12/30/2024

### Format: 📕 Print

This one was a big of a dark horse. It’s more of a management book that also chronicles key moments in the life Pixar as a company. _How did they become the Pixar we know today?_ This book answers that question in **spades**.

There’s a lot great little nuggets to digest about creativity in your personal and professional life, as well as some very specific ideas about business structure that clearly works for a creative company. _I found a lot of it relevant and timely to my own new business that’s starting this year_.
