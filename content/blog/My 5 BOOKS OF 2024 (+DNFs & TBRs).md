# *I DON’T READ MUCH* 📖 *(CLEARLY)*
![SpongeBob reading a field guide](assets/spongebob.gif)

_Yes, you read that title correctly_—I read a whole **five** books this year. _For **me**_, that’s as good as it gets. I also had a handful I didn’t finish, and I have some great books to read in 2025.

I tend to go for difficult books, either in length, subject matter, or both. I also tend to stick to nonfiction, specifically in the areas of biography, social science, psychology, anthropology, and other similar disciplines. I love learning more about real people, things, and ideas.

By the way: All but one of these books were ones I borrowed from the library. If you don’t already use [Libby](https://libbyapp.com/), what are you doing? It’s one of the best things your local library has available to you. One of the perks of going back to community college has been the wider access to more audiobooks, which I think is definitely the best format for me now.

Let’s get into my list! ⬇️

# _**BOOKS I FINISHED**_ ✅
![The Will to Change - bell hooks](assets/the-will-to-change.jpg)

## _The Will to Change by Bell Hooks_

### Finished: 03/07/2024

### **Format: 💻 E-book**

This was my **first** Bell Hooks book, but it most certainly _won’t be my last_. What can be said about her that hasn’t already been said? This is a fantastic read to understand her unique perspective of men in the discussion of **patriarchy**, **masculinity**, and **relationships**.

She doesn’t attack anyone—she points out how many of the power structures in place benefit a select few, and how men performing that brand of masculinity are also victims in that pursuit. I can’t wait to read more of her incredible writing.

### **5 (patriarchies sucking real bad for everyone) out of 5**

![Unmasking Autism - Devon Price](assets/unmasking-autism.jpg)
## _Unmasking Autism by Devon Price, PhD_

### Finished: 05/13/2024

### Format: 💻 E-book

In my ongoing self-exploration, I wanted to get some perspective on autism. Dr. Price discusses the difficulties of late diagnosis, how to find community, and many other frameworks _to understand what autism isn’t as much as what it really is_. As you would expect, a book written by people with autism for people with autism has plenty of worksheets and other resources to use as a companion with the book.

As an undiagnosed, but likely autistic person, I found this to be very _insightful_ and _valuable_, even if I know a lot of the things I read in this may not be things I’m ready to accept just yet. **These things take time.**

### 5 (special interests that aren’t just trains) out of 5

![Elon Musk - Walter Isaacson](assets/elon-musk.jpg)
## _Elon Musk by Walter Isaacson_

### Finished: 08/11/2024

### Format: 🎧 Audiobook

As one of the richest people that’s ever existed, Elon is…an _interesting_ _person_ to say the least. Isaacson paints an in-depth picture as always, but his track record of genius worship doesn’t lend itself to authentic objectivity. It did come out a bit before his recent political…_“pivot”_, but I can’t help but feel like it was a strategic move on Musk’s part (both the pivot and book release, to be clear).

This has given me a couple of different conclusions: **soft ball journalism has no word limit**. **Information, no matter how objective, can be biased in _subtle_ ways**. At the end of the day, I learned more about who Elon is (and isn’t), but I didn’t find much of it very surprising.

### 4 (hundred billion dollars, but somehow zero actual friends) out of 5

![Comedy Drama - Bob Odenkirk](assets/comedy-drama.jpg)
## _Comedy Comedy Comedy Drama by Bob Odenkirk_

### Finished: 10/21/2024

### Format: 🎧 Audiobook

Like a lot of people my age, my first time seeing Bob in anything was his amazing work as Saul Goodman in Breaking Bad and the subsequent prequel series Better Call Saul. As the title suggests, his passion and focus has been **much** more _skewed_ towards comedy throughout his career, and learning about his connection to many different comedy legends that I knew of—and a lot that I didn’t—was a super fun journey to be on.

_His written voice read by his spoken voice is a real treat, and I don’t think anyone else could have done it justice._

### 4 (times I had no idea who he was talking about) out of 5
![Creativity, Inc. - Ed Catmull](assets/creativity-inc.jpg)
## _Creativity, Inc. by Ed Catmull_

### Finished: 12/30/2024

### Format: 📕 Print

This one was a big of a dark horse. It’s more of a management book that also chronicles key moments in the life Pixar as a company. _How did they become the Pixar we know today?_ This book answers that question in **spades**.

There’s a lot great little nuggets to digest about creativity in your personal and professional life, as well as some very specific ideas about business structure that clearly works for a creative company. _I found a lot of it relevant and timely to my own new business that’s starting this year_.

# _**BOOKS I DIDN’T**_ ❎ _**(DNFs)**_
![A Promised Land - Barack Obama](assets/a-promised-land.jpg)
## _A Promised Land by Barack Obama_

### Format: 🎧 Audiobook

This was much less focused on Obama’s personal life than I had hoped. The inside baseball of politics is honestly not what I was interested in, and it was probably just too relevant to the moment I was living through (that being the lead up to the 2024 Presidential Election—_**oof**_).

But hearing someone in the White House speak with passion will be **sorely missed** for yet another four years. I may come back to this one, but it may be awhile before then.
![Be Useful - Arnold Schwarzenegger](assets/be-useful.jpg)
## _Be Useful by Arnold Schwarzenegger_

### Format: 🎧 Audiobook

Arnie is one of the most fascinating people to ever grace the silver screen, but I just _**didn’t find myself needing his words of inspiration**_ at this particular moment. He’s speaking to people who have no plan in particular, and needing some basic guidance on how to make one, and move in that direction with a specific vision.

It’s **fantastic** advice, wrapped in fun stories from his very interesting life, but isn’t really for me at this moment in my life. That being said, there’s still a good few nuggets left, so we may revisit this one to finish it up soon.
![Cinema Speculation - Quentin Tarantino](assets/cinema-speculation.jpg)
## _Cinema Speculation by Quentin Tarantino_

### Format: 🎧 Audiobook

Quentin is definitely a character in his own right. Like a lot of bios I’ve read, he focuses a lot of his energy and attention on the thing he loves—cinema—and _paints a picture of his life around that **central focus**_. This one is chalk full of name dropping that I had a hard time following, but I do wanna finish it, since I actually paid for it (lol).

Similar to Obama’s book, _you have to respect the passion_ that he has for his craft and the work he’s put into it, and I want to give the time and attention it deserves.
![Against Technoableism - Ashley Shew](assets/against-technoableism.jpg)
## _Against Technoableism by Ashley Shew_

### Format: 💻 E-book

This is an exploration into the issues with ableism, disability, and how many of the technologies that are designed to support them aren’t designed by the true experts: **the users themselves**. On top of that, I found out that the writer of this book is a professor at Virginia Tech, which I grew up right down the road from.

There are a lot of different facets to this particular area that I haven’t really gotten into as much, so I’m hoping to revisit this one soon.
![American Prometheus - Kai Bird & Martin J. Sherwin](assets/american-prometheus.jpg)
## _American Prometheus by Kai Bird and Martin J Sherwin_

### Format: 📕 Print +🎧 Audiobook

After the massive success of Oppenheimer, my parents purchased the **equally massive**, Pulitzer Prize winning book that inspired Nolan to make that movie. While it’s an interesting time of history, I think I find more interest in more contemporary history. _It’s very hard to find him relatable_, both because of his bygone era of living, and for the fact that he was the kind of person who was never without, **even during the Great Depression**.

_Oh_, and _**he created the atomic bomb**_, or heavily contributed to it, as it were. This one may stay on this list, though. It just will take some doing, cause this is one long book.

# _**BOOKS I WANNA ⏳ (TBRs)**_
![Cultish - Amanda Montell](assets/cultish.jpg)
## _Cultish by Amanda Montell_

### Format: 🎧 Audiobook

This will be my first finished book of this year, and is one of my favorites I’ve read in quite awhile. Imagine a linguist breaks down the cultural significance of the language of cults, from the ones that end in death to the ones that end in shredded abs.

_You don’t need to imagine that_—**this book is as exactly as nerdy as I had hoped**. I’ll report back on this one soon.
![Shogun - James Clavell](assets/shogun.jpg)
## _Shōgun, Part One by James Clavell_

### Format: 💻 E-book

After watching this absolutely fantastic show, I wanted to dive into the pair of novels that inspired it. While this is fiction, it’s well researched fiction with some creative and dramatic liberties taken, so about as close to nonfiction as fiction can get. Did I say that f-word enough times? _**Not that one.**_

Count this as exactly one book on my list that’s not nonfiction, but may as well be. _I didn’t say I wanted to change my reading preferences, did I?_
![iWoz - Steve Wozniak](assets/iwoz.jpg)
## _iWoz by Steve Wozniak_

### Format: 📕 Print

I found a copy of this in a used book store after reading Oscar Isaacson’s exhausti—I mean exhaustive account of Steve Jobs’ life (_just kidding, it was an interesting read_). I can’t tell you how refreshing it was to see that something from Woz’s autobiographical perspective existed, and seemed to carry a more upbeat tone to it to boot.

**Sly computer jargon humor aside**, the origins of personal computers as we know them are not attributed to Woz nearly enough, and I look forward to learning more about him.
![README.txt - Chelsea Manning](assets/readme.jpg)
## _README.txt by Chelsea Manning_

### Format: 🎧 Audiobook

Chelsea Manning is not a name that I knew before, but when searching for more niche reads, I stumbled across her memoir. She is an activist in both her **personal** and **professional** lives, as a trans woman and a whistleblower within the intelligence community.

As our society’s connection to technology evolves, and we also deal with the push-pull between it and so-called _“traditional values”_, her voice is an important one to hear.
![Doppleganger - Naomi Klein](assets/doppleganger.jpg)
## _Doppelganger by Naomi Klein_

### Format: 🎧 Audiobook

I first read Naomi Klein’s book The Shock Doctrine as a confused college kid in a class that flew right over my head. _The contents of it made me reconsider my life and the world around me._

Fast forward 10 years, and Naomi found herself in the same position of dealing with the unexpected. Someone who looked liker her had _**only that in common**_, possessing the opposite values, beliefs, and views on the world, and _**threatened to dismantle hers**_.

What even is a wheelhouse? Maybe I can read about that. _**Anyway**_—that’s my book list for ’24-’25!
